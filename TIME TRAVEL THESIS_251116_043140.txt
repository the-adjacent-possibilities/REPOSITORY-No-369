\section{Historical Overview of Time Travel in Physics}
\label{sec:historical}

The concept of time travel has, for over a century, persisted as both a locus of scientific investigation and philosophical controversy. Foundational works within General Relativity, such as the solutions posited by Gödel \cite{goedel1949} and Tipler \cite{tipler1974}, suggest the theoretical permissibility of Closed Timelike Curves (CTCs), despite their reliance on conditions considered physically unattainable. The ubiquitous presence of causality paradoxes within these formulations renders time travel a problematic, if not outright forbidden, proposition under conventional physical law.
